OneDayOnly

UI/UX Design & development for an e-commerce site

About

OneDayOnly is one of South Africa's most popular online stores. Beginning with customer research, I worked on designs that improved the user experience, overhauled the visual style, and finally participated in the rebuilding of the site's front-end.

Project date
2018-2020
Website link
new.onedayonly.co.za

The problem

OneDayOnly has had a successful online store for years, but first time users were often sceptical as the original website's dated design resulted in a lack of trust. The company has a great, quirky brand which pulls through in their marketing campaigns, yet was lost in the old website.

Some of the problems I aimed to solve during this project were:

  1. The design needed to reassure users that the deals are legitimate and trustworthy
  2. Users should be encouraged to make spontaneous purchases, with reduced friction during the checkout process
  3. The quirkiness of the brand needed to be pull through to the online store, creating a fun experience for users
The original OneDayOnly shop design

It's not a scam: Conveying legitimacy through design

When it comes to online shopping, customers look for visual cues to make sure that they can trust the brand enough to give up their credit card details.

By removing the grey background and minimising the clutter caused by the busy side bar, The banner is a bold colour to highlight the colourful brand, while the site itself remains clean and white. Improved usability: Prices are now always visible, and not only on hover.
