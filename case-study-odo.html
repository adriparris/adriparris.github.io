The problem

OneDayOnly has a successful online store, but first time users are often sceptical as the original design does not look professional or trustworthy. The company has a great, quirky brand which pulls through in their marketing campaigns, but does not show up well on their current site.

The main problems I aimed to solve in this project were:

  1. Find a way to display the products in a way that makes the site appear legitimate
  2. Encourage users to shop spontaneously by reducing friction in the checkout process
  3. Bring in the quirkiness of the brand throughout the site
New OneDayOnly shop design

Improve appearance of legitimacy

I removed the dated grey background and minimised the clutter caused by the busy side bar. The banner is a bold colour to highlight the colourful brand, while the site itself remains clean and white. Improved usability: Prices are now always visible, and not only on hover.
